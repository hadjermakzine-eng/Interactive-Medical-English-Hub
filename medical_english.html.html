🔤 Medical Prefixes & Suffixes

Prefixes: hyper- (high), hypo- (low), tachy- (fast), brady- (slow), dys- (abnormal), intra- (within). Suffixes: -itis (inflammation), -ectomy (removal), -algia (pain), -emia (blood condition).

📌 Preposition Activity – AT / ON / IN + Medical Prepositions

Grammar rule reminder: Use AT for specific times (at 9 AM), ON for days/dates (on Monday), IN for longer periods (in the morning, in June). Also practice clinical prepositions: admitted to, discharged from, allergic to, treat with, present with.
