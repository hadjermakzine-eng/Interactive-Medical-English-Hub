## 📚 Full Manual Activities

This repository contains resources from the complete *English is the Microphone* manual, including:

- **7 clinical episodes** with dialogues (hospital cafeteria, ER, ward rounds, lectures)
- **Vocabulary tables** (English terms + French equivalents + pronunciation)
- **Grammar corners** (prepositions, simple past, future with `will`, present continuous)
- **Pronunciation drills** (–ed endings, **-itis** stress, exploding T, tongue twisters)
- **False friends list** (40+ French–English traps)
- **Phrasal verb tables** (clinical actions, patient events, communication)
- **Clinical documentation** (SOAP notes, SBAR handover, BRAIN consent protocol)
- **Medical abbreviations** (English → French)
- **Prefixes & suffixes** (hyper‑, hypo‑, –itis, –ectomy, –algia)
- **Lab values & number pronunciation** (point, over, large numbers)
- **Self‑assessment** (diagnostic test with answer key)

 🕵️‍♀️ Interactive Game: Find Dr. Noura

**A Medical English game where you unlock clues by answering clinical questions – mentioned last but equally important!**


How it works
- You are given **6 Medical English questions** (false friends, phrasal verbs, grammar, idioms).
- Each correct answer **unlocks one clue** on the evidence board.
- Clues progressively reveal **where Dr. Noura is** – but you must figure it out yourself.
- After all 6 clues, a **British‑themed celebration** appears with a double‑decker bus, Big Ben, and the full location:  
  **Royal College of Surgeons of England, London (Urology Royal Symposium).**


 What you’ll practice
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| False friends | `actuellement` → **currently** (not actually) ; `sensible` → **sensitive** |
| Phrasal verbs | `rule out` (exclude a diagnosis), `black out` (lose consciousness) |
| Medical idioms | `under the weather`, `bitter pill to swallow`, `code blue` |
| Grammar & prepositions | `on` Monday `at` 9 AM, past tense of irregular verbs |

> ✅ No installation – just a browser. Works on desktop and mobile.
